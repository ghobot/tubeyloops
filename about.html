Tubeyloops is an open source project that pushes the limits of video playback in the web browser. It attempts to bring the functionality and experience of expensive beat and video sequencers to a web app powered by the open links generated by HTML5's video element. In this current iteration, it relies heavily on the work of patternsketch.com, a dynamic beat sequencer that also looks to experiment what is possible for creation, collaboration and performance in state of the art web browsers.

One thing to note about tubeyloops is that it relies on links that define the concept of the open web. What is the open web? It is the concerted effort to maintain for the web user transparency of how websites, and web apps function. It is the web not locked behind proprietary, trademarked and copyrighted technologies like the popular video codecs and containers like flash and h264. With this openness, and a healthy exercising of fair use, a web surfer has all the tools to shape and remix the web in new and innovative ways.
